Train our Autoencoder

Now that we have our 3D trajectory of 8 atoms (that is to say 8*3=24 input dimensions), we can implement our neural network and train it with those data. Our goal is to reduce the dimension from 22 to 2. Our neural netword is made up of an encoder and a decoder layers. The encoder contains two fully connected layers, of respective dimensions 40 and 2. All activation function are tanh. The decoder is symmetric to the encoder.


Here is a link to our notebook. We use the adam optimization algorithm. The model is trained with 300 epochs. We see above that the loss of our neural network drop drastically during training. The loss is below 74% which is really good. That means our autoencoder correctly restitute the initial trajectory after the dimension reduction and that our weight is supposed to be well learned. What is an autoencoder?

Autoencoder is an unsupervised artificial neural network that learns how to efficiently compress and encode data then learns how to reconstruct the data back from the reduced encoded representation to a representation that is as close to the original input as possible.
